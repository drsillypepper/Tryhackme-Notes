A is a device within a network responsible for determining what traffic is allowed to enter and exit. Its the gatekeeper of networking

The firewalls can be configured to **permit** or **deny** the network traffic going forward from and to the network.

common factors
- Where the traffic is coming from? (has the been told to accept/deny traffic from a specific network?)
- Where is the traffic going to? (has the been told to accept/deny traffic destined for a specific network?)
- What port is the traffic for? (has the been told to accept/deny traffic destined for port 80 only?)
- What protocol is the traffic using? (has the been told to accept/deny traffic that is , or both?)


## Stateful firewall
This type of uses the entire information from a connection; rather than inspecting an individual packet, this determines the behaviour of a device **based upon the entire connection**.
- compute heavy, due to its dynamic nature
- if the connnecttion is bad, it can block the device

## stateless firewall.
It uses static set of rules to filter out individual packets. Bad packet that is not acceptable may not mean that the entire device gets blocked.
- good firewalls when it comes down to receiving large amounts of traffic from a set of hosts.
- stateless firewalls are dumb as fuck

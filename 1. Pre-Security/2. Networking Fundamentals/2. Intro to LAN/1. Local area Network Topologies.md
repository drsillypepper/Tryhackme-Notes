### Star Topology
- all devices are connected individually to the central networking unit such as switch or hub. 
- the most costly out of them all. 
- most reliable, though if the center fails then everything does.
- Unfortunately, the more the network scales, the more maintenance is required to keep the network functional. This increased dependence on maintenance can also make troubleshooting faults much harder.


## Bus topology
- main concept is backbone cable in which all devices register onto.
- collisions inside the central bus is common
- one of the most cost efficient topologies to setup.

## Ring Topology
- The ring topology (also known as token topology) boasts some similarities. Devices such as computers are connected directly to each other to form a loop, meaning that there is little cabling required and less dependence on dedicated hardware such as within a star topology.
- only one direction where data flows.
- if one quest node breaks in this topology, communication fails

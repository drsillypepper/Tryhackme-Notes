
ARP (address resolution protocol) is a technology responsible for allowing devices to identify themselves on a network.
- it associates mac address with ip addresses on the network
- each device keeps log of mac addresses associated with other devices

When communication occurs on a network
- they send a broadcast to the entire network searching the device they want to connect to.
- if they have already know of the info of the device they can just look up arp tables to find the devices ip address

## How does arp work?
In the context of arp cache, this cache stores the identifiers of other devices on the network.

In order to map ip and mac addresses together, there are 2 types of messages on the network to handle that.
- arp request (ask for all devices whether any of them has the given mac address)
- arp reply (the given device that has the mac address replies with its ip address)
